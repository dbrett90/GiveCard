Paiton: Meet the Team Mondays

Paiton: Meet the Team Mondays

Hello, I’m Paiton! Thank you for visiting our website and for your interest in GiveCard. I grew up outside of Boston with parents who instilled in me a desire to consistently reflect on what I am doing to help others. I am currently an incoming senior at Boston College majoring in Biology and Finance. In my free time, I teach yoga and HIIT classes, run and hike, work as a wellness coach, and listen to music and audiobooks galore. Before coming to BC, I spent a year living abroad in East Africa and Peru. In East Africa, I worked with local NGOs which strongly shaped my views on what makes a powerful nonprofit organization. In Peru, I completed my 200-hr yoga teacher training, which focused specifically on yoga as a form of community outreach.

GiveCard is truly a team effort - we are all extremely passionate about the organization and chip in wherever we can. With this being said, my primary focus of late has been financial education. Financial literacy is an under-covered topic across many communities, and a topic rarely addressed in relation to our homeless community. Our vision for GiveCard is a platform which enables sustainable change. We want to empower GiveCard clients with the tools they need to overcome homelessness and maintain a stable financial life thereafter. I recently completed my NFEC financial educator certification to help me create a thorough program for GiveCard clients. Topics covered will include financial psychology; savings, expenses & budgeting; account management; loans & debt; credit; income; economic & government influences; risk management & insurance; investing & personal financial planning; and education & skill development.


Why GiveCard?


Innovation should not be limited to for-profit companies. The world has largely gone digital. People pay via Venmo instead of cash, deposit checks with a banking app rather than a bank, and some restaurants and businesses have even banned cash. GiveCard aims to bring our homeless community into the digital age. It has become less common for people to carry cash, but very few people leave home without their phone. Innovative ideas such as these cannot come about without a cohesive team. The GiveCard team unit is composed of individuals with a diverse skill set, all united by an unwavering vision of what GiveCard could do for the people that we serve.

Lastly, there are many complex problems that I care about in this world. Yet homelessness in our country that has the largest share of the global economy is particularly pressing. As a society, we need to examine what systemic change we can create to keep our fellow humans from living without shelter.
