Andrew: Meet the Team Mondays

“Alright, what’s 234 times 26?”
“What’s the capital of Oregon”
“What do you need to break in order to use?”

6,084.  Salem.  An egg.  No matter what we asked, Charlie needed only pause, look up for a short period of time, and then respond with the correct answer.  Charlie was a fifth-grade camper at Today’s Youth Matter, a summer camp for inner city youth that I and many of my high school classmates were volunteering at.  Despite his age, Charlie was studying advanced Calculus and aspired to work at NASA.  Charlie also grew up homeless.

I was born and raised in the Bay Area, an innovation and entrepreneurship hub which is also home to some of the highest homeless populations in the United States.  While our sports franchises, tech companies and universities have enjoyed a positive national spotlight, many turn a blind eye on our homeless communities. Our high cost of living and fiercely competitive job market means many are pushed onto the streets and into a vicious cycle of applying for jobs while searching for food, shelter and adequate clothing.

Growing up, I’ve witnessed society’s stigma against the homeless.  Words like “druggie”, “tweaker”, and “crackhead” are used interchangeably to describe the homeless, grouping the population together into something alien and deserving of poor circumstance.  When I see someone who is homeless, I see Charlie, with his incredible passion and ambition, fighting a rigged game. When I moved to Boston, I realized these issues were not regional, and that something needed to change.


Why GiveCard?


I joined GiveCard in November of 2018 after meeting Lurein at a Boston College Shea Center Networking event.  Apart from Lurein’s infectious passion for the idea, my main attraction towards GiveCard came primarily from its emphasis on destigmatizing homelessness.  GiveCard is a service oriented, fintech-powered nonprofit that helps break down the barriers created by a cashless society.  Our team figured out that many today hesitate to donate to homeless individuals for one of two reasons:  1) they do not carry physical cash in an amount that they would be willing to part with and 2) they do not want to further an addiction or contribute towards a negative habit.  GiveCard is a solution to both of those hesitations, and allows interconnection and generosity to flourish.

Aside from the mission, I absolutely love working on a team of hardworking and passionate individuals.  We started as a group of mission-driven BC students (which we still are) and developed rapidly.  I can honestly say that working alongside some of my best friends makes dedicating any amount of time easy, and that I am inspired by each member of the team.  I cannot wait for the milestones to come!
