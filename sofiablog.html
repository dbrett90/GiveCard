Sofia: Meet the Team Mondays

Sofia: Meet the Team Mondays

Every time my family drives to my grandma’s house from the airport in Buenos Aires, Argentina, we pass by an area blocked in by three momentous blocks of cement created for highways. I remember distinctly passing by there in 2008 seeing a couple of people experiencing homelessness. Most recently, in 2019 at this same spot there are now entire buildings, a mini city, made from metal scraps and cardboard. It’s no doubt that homelessness is a problem everywhere in the world, but seeing it grow to this extend is enough for me to have to do something about it.

I was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina and have lived in various places through my childhood, moving from city to city until my family recently settled in Lexington, Massachusetts—only a short drive from Boston College. When not working on GiveCard, I am dancing Bollywood with my SASA friends or trying out new foods.


Why GiveCard?


I have grown to live by the quote, “We can’t help everyone, but everyone can help someone.” I truly believe that everyone in life deserves a chance and some may just need a helping hand to realize that chance—which is why I chose to join GiveCard. With GiveCard, apart from a team that I consider family, I saw an opportunity to make real, lasting change in my community. We may fall on hard times in our lives, but that does not mean we can’t get back up and I want to fight the stigma that those persons facing homelessness are helpless—that could not be further from the truth.

I joined GiveCard as Head of Growth in early 2018 and am responsible for creating a network of shelters, resources for our program and discovering distribution channels for our debit cards.

GiveCard is a resource that will inflict real, useful change in the world of homelessness. I would say having a team that shares these ideas, passions and are genuinely some of the best people I’ve ever met is also a plus. In joining the team, I strive to be at the forefront of social work that can truly help someone who has fallen on hard times and strengthen our community as a whole.
